## Reflection

### What are the characteristics of phishing pages (e.g., a wrong URL)?

Phishing pages are designed to look like legitimate websites and trick users into inputing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, or banking details. Common indicators include suspicious or misspelled URLs, missing HTTPS protection, spelling mistakes, unexpected login requests, and unusual domain names.

### How would you protect yourself from such an attack?

To protect myself from phishing attacks, I verify the website URL before entering credentials, avoid opening suspicious links received through email or messages, and check whether the website uses HTTPS. I also use multi-factor authentication (MFA).

### Why do modern sites make such attacks more difficult?

Modern websites use various security mechanisms to reduce the effectiveness of phishing attacks. HTTPS certificates help users verify website, while browsers often display warnings for suspicious or deceptive websites. Many services also use multi-factor authentication, making stolen passwords less useful to attackers.
